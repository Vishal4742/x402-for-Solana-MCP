Agentic Engineering Grant Shortlist

User profile: infra builder, wants a 1-2 week MVP, strongest wedge is agent tooling, and the first reachable buyers are founders shipping MVPs.

Recommendation

Build Agent Treasury Guard for Solana MVPs. It solves a real founder fear right now: letting agents touch money without giving them unlimited keys. The product can launch narrow, piggyback on existing Solana wallet primitives, and still uses crypto for something genuinely native: programmable spend control over onchain assets.

Fastest credible MVP Clear buyer pain Strong crypto necessity Good grant story

Shortlist

Rank #1 • Winner

Agent Treasury Guard for Solana MVPs

Give AI agents scoped Solana wallets with spend caps, recipient allowlists, transaction simulation, and human escalation so founders can automate actions without exposing the whole treasury.

Why it winsSharpest pain, fastest wedge, and easiest story to explain to grant reviewers.
Why crypto is necessaryProgrammable wallets, onchain balances, and policy-enforced settlement are the product, not decoration.
Why not Web2Stripe-style policy engines do not control open Solana assets or compose with wallets, token accounts, and onchain programs.
Kill this ideaIf founders refuse autonomous spending entirely and keep every transaction manual, this collapses into a dashboard feature.
Bear caseWallet infra vendors could absorb this as a feature. Survives because founders need a tighter agent-first opinionated layer than generic wallet APIs.
ScoresFounder fit 3, MVP speed 2, distribution clarity 2, market pull 2, revenue path 2.
Direct competitorsProco, Crossmint wallet infrastructure, generic programmable wallet vendors.
Adjacent substitutesManual multisig approvals, spreadsheet budgets, Turnkey-style backend wallet controls, internal scripts.
OSS landscapeSolana Agent Kit and assetCLI prove builders want agents to transact, but they do not center spend policy and safety as the core wedge.
Rank #2

x402 for Solana MCP Servers

A drop-in billing layer that lets founders charge agent tools, MCP servers, and APIs in USDC on Solana per request, per session, or per workflow without wiring Stripe, subscriptions, or manual invoicing.

Why it survivesMachine-to-machine payments are a legitimate crypto wedge and the monetization story is cleaner than most agent products.
Why crypto is necessaryOpen wallet-based settlement and micropayments between software actors are the core value, especially across untrusted counterparties.
Why it lostThe market is heating up fast and standards may compress differentiation before you get distribution.
Kill this ideaIf buyers just want card billing or monthly SaaS invoices, the crypto layer becomes optional and the wedge weakens.
Bear casex402 becomes infrastructure and your product gets squeezed. Survives if you focus narrowly on Solana founders shipping MCP-based tools now.
ScoresFounder fit 2, MVP speed 2, distribution clarity 2, market pull 2, revenue path 3.
Direct competitorsFourOhTwo, AION SDK, any wallet-native agent payment rail.
Adjacent substitutesStripe subscriptions, API keys with prepaid credits, manual invoicing, enterprise procurement.
OSS landscapeEmerging payment standards and Solana agent tooling reduce build time, but there is room for a founder-focused implementation layer.
Rank #3

Onchain Agent Receipts and Reputation

Issue verifiable receipts for agent actions, tool calls, and task completion on Solana so founders can audit what agents did, prove service quality, and eventually route work by agent reputation.

Why it survivesIt is a real cross-app coordination primitive and fits the broader agent economy thesis.
Why crypto is necessaryPortable identity, verifiable state, and shared reputation across parties are much stronger on an open ledger than in siloed logs.
Why it lostPain is less urgent than wallet safety or billing, and founders shipping MVPs do not yet wake up desperate for agent reputation graphs.
Kill this ideaIf nobody outside your app reads or trusts the receipts, you are just writing expensive logs.
Bear caseToo early and too abstract for first buyers. Survives because a narrow receipt layer for payouts or contractor agents could still land.
ScoresFounder fit 2, MVP speed 2, distribution clarity 1, market pull 1, revenue path 1.
Direct competitorsSAID Protocol, ERC-8004-style onchain agent identity projects.
Adjacent substitutesSigned webhooks, app logs, internal audit trails, centralized reputation systems.
OSS landscapeIdentity registries exist, but a Solana-native receipts layer tied to practical founder workflows is still less defined.

Why the winner beats the other two

  • It addresses a pain founders feel before scale: agents touching treasury is scary immediately.
  • It is integration-first. You can build a credible MVP on top of existing wallet and Solana agent tooling instead of inventing a new protocol.
  • It has a simple demo path for the grant: create policy, run agent action, block bad transfer, approve safe one.
  • It naturally leads to paid expansion: per wallet, per policy, or team plan.

2-week MVP shape for the winner

Week 1

  • Create agent wallet and budget policy.
  • Support allowlist and per-tx spend cap in USDC or SOL.
  • Simulate transfers and swaps before execution.
  • Expose one API and one simple founder dashboard.

Week 2

  • Add human approval fallback for risky actions.
  • Show action history and blocked attempts.
  • Integrate one agent framework or MCP server example.
  • Publish demo video and starter repo.

Suggested one-line grant pitch

Agent Treasury Guard lets founders ship Solana-native AI agents faster by giving each agent a programmable wallet with spend caps, allowlists, and human fail-safes instead of raw private keys.
